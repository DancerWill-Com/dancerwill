About

Artist.
Athlete.
Storyteller.

William Crimmins began his dance training at age eight, driven by an instinct for movement that quickly revealed itself as something more — a calling. Over nine years, he has built a technically rigorous and artistically expansive foundation across Modern, Jazz, and Ballet, with a competitive record that reflects both discipline and distinction.

As a participant in the Blake McGrath Mentorship Program, Will worked directly under one of the industry's most dynamic choreographic voices. At the Joffrey Spectrum Dance Intensive, he trained alongside elite peers and faculty, deepening his classical and contemporary technique in equal measure.

Will has had the privilege of working with renowned choreographers whose influence continues to shape his artistic identity — instilling not only technical excellence but a commitment to the kind of storytelling that makes movement matter.

In the fall of 2026, Will will begin his formal conservatory training at Pace University's Sands College of Performing Arts, pursuing a Bachelor of Fine Arts in Dance — the next chapter in a career already defined by intention, rigor, and creative ambition.

Award-winning downtown hotel situated within the LECOM HarborCenter complex, connected by walking bridge directly to KeyBank Center. Rooms and suites feature local artwork, platform beds, spa-inspired bathrooms, and stunning views of Lake Erie and the Buffalo waterfront. The on-site Panorama on Seven restaurant offers seasonal American cuisine and handcrafted cocktails with waterfront views. Steps from Canalside, the Buffalo Convention Center (Nickel City Con!), and the city's best dining.

95 Main St · Downtown Buffalo · 205 rooms & suites · Waterfront views · On-site dining · Fitness center · ~30 min to Lancaster

Where to Eat

Dining in Buffalo

Buffalo's food scene has quietly become one of the most exciting in the Northeast — far beyond wings. From James Beard-recognized chefs to the iconic originals, here's the short list.

Upscale Mexican · Black Rock

Las Puertas

Chef Victor Parra Gonzalez is a James Beard Award semi-finalist. Housemade tortillas, exceptional octopus, dishes that look almost too beautiful to eat. One of Buffalo's very best tables.

385 Rhode Island St · Reservations recommended

New American · Downtown

Marble + Rye

Wood-fired cooking with a cult-following burger, exceptional bone marrow, seasonal vegetables, and a top-tier whiskey list. Downtown Buffalo's most talked-about contemporary spot.

112 Genesee St · Downtown

Classic Buffalo · Allentown

Left Bank

Chef David Albright's beloved institution. Eclectic menu pulling from Italian, French, and New American. The outdoor patio is one of the city's most coveted summer dining spots.

511 Rhode Island St · Allentown

Italian · Downtown

Ristorante Lombardo

High-end Italian in a stylish atmosphere — classic veal and pasta with an excellent wine list. One of Buffalo's most enduring fine dining institutions and consistently well regarded.

1198 Hertel Ave

Wings — The Original · Midtown

Anchor Bar

The birthplace of the Buffalo wing in 1964. Non-negotiable for first-time visitors. Teressa Bellissimo's original recipe still holds up. A piece of American culinary history.

1047 Main St · A Buffalo rite of passage

Wings — Locals' Pick · Sheridan Dr

Duff's Famous Wings

Anchor Bar's long-standing rival for Buffalo wing supremacy. Many locals swear by Duff's. The original Sheridan Drive location has classic tavern energy and devoted regulars.

3651 Sheridan Dr · Near Airport

Explore

Western New York

Buffalo and the surrounding region offer far more than most visitors expect. From one of the world's great natural wonders to a world-class art museum, a summer weekend here rewards exploration.

Natural Wonder · 30 min away

Niagara Falls

Six million cubic feet of water per minute. One of the most awe-inspiring sights on earth. Experience it from Niagara Falls State Park — the oldest state park in the US — via Maid of the Mist boat tour, the Cave of the Winds walkways, or the Prospect Point overlook. Plan 3–4 hours minimum.

Maid of the Mist · Cave of the Winds · Niagara Scenic Trolley · Goat Island · Rainbow Air Helicopter Tours

World-Class Art · Delaware Park

AKG Art Museum

The Albright-Knox Gallery — now AKG — is one of the oldest and most distinguished contemporary art museums in the world. Recently expanded with a stunning new addition. Picasso, Warhol, Pollock — serious work in an extraordinary building.

1285 Elmwood Ave · Delaware Park

Waterfront · Downtown

Canalside

Buffalo's revitalized waterfront district hosts over 1,000 annual events. In summer: concerts, kayaking on the Buffalo River, sailing on the Spirit of Buffalo, and outdoor dining. The perfect afternoon before dinner downtown.

1 Naval Park Cove · Downtown Buffalo

Architecture · North Buffalo

Frank Lloyd Wright's Martin House

The Darwin D. Martin House Complex is considered Wright's greatest Prairie-style masterpiece — a National Historic Landmark offering guided tours. Essential for anyone who appreciates architecture or design.

125 Jewett Pkwy · North Buffalo

History · Old Fort Niagara

Old Fort Niagara

A strategically vital fort at the mouth of the Niagara River, dating to the French colonial period. Living history demonstrations, stunning Lake Ontario views, and a fascinating slice of American and Canadian military history.

Fort Niagara State Park · Youngstown, NY

Shopping & Neighborhood · Elmwood

Elmwood Village

Named one of America's 10 Best Neighborhoods by the American Planning Association. A walkable stretch of independent boutiques, galleries, coffee shops, and excellent restaurants — the heart of Buffalo's creative community.

Elmwood Ave corridor · Near Delaware Park

Day Trip · Ontario, Canada

Niagara-on-the-Lake

Often called the prettiest town in Canada, NOTL is a beautifully preserved 19th-century village just across the border — roughly 30 minutes from Niagara Falls. Dozens of world-class wineries, the acclaimed Shaw Festival theatre company, flower-lined streets, boutique shops, and a vibrant farm-to-table dining scene. Come for the wine, stay for the charm.

~1 hr from Buffalo · Passport required · Visit NOTL →

Day Trip · Finger Lakes, NY

Seneca Lake Wine Trail

New York's largest and most visited wine trail winds 70+ miles around Seneca Lake — the deepest of the Finger Lakes — with 30+ award-winning member wineries. Known for world-class Riesling, Cabernet Franc, and Chardonnay, all grown in the lake's unique cool climate. Pair the tasting trail with a stop at dramatic Watkins Glen State Park for a complete Finger Lakes day.

~2 hrs from Buffalo · No passport needed · Plan Your Visit →
